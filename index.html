Independent Redistricting Commissions

and the fight against

Gerrymandering

Every 10 years, a nationwide census takes place to count every resident in the United States. This helps lawmakers understand the population growth (or decline) across the nation's 50 states.


With each decade's population change, the number of representatives granted to each state is adjusted in order to preserve the proportional nature of the U.S. House of Representatives. This process of reassigning the number of representatives is called reapportionment.

Along with potentially adjusting the number of Congressional districts after reapportionment, each state must also redraw their districts so each district has roughly the same number of people.
